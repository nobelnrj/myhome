<objective>
Give the kitchen its persistence spine: author SchemaV11 as an additive superset of
SchemaV10 (which Phase 18 shipped) that copies every existing class verbatim and adds
two brand-new @Models — `PantryItem` (name, quantity, unit, lowStockThreshold,
restockQuantity, category, notes — KTCH-01) and `ShoppingListItem` (manual shopping
rows — KTCH-03) — both carrying `syncID`/`updatedAt` and conforming to `SyncStamped`
from birth so they flow through the Phase 18 sync engine (KTCH-04). Wire the V10→V11
migration stage, flip every typealias (now 14) AND every SyncStamped conformance
extension to V11 atomically (STAB-08), and prove it with fixture migration tests.

Design decision (recorded here, consumed by Plans 03/04): the shopping list is
DERIVED + MANUAL. Low/out-of-stock rows are computed from PantryItem state at render
time (never materialized), so two phones can never mint duplicate "auto" rows that
the merge engine must reconcile; `ShoppingListItem` rows exist ONLY for
manually-added extras. Check-off restock is unambiguous because a derived row IS its
PantryItem. Quantities are `Double`, not Decimal — pantry amounts are not money
(precedent: `parseConfidence: Double` in the schema); DTOs pass them through
unchanged in Plan 02.

Purpose: kitchen models that are syncable from their first row means Phase 20 never
needs a backfill migration, and the merge engine (18-03) treats kitchen entities
exactly like every other synced record.

Output: SchemaV11 live in the production container, kitchen tables queryable,
migration + STAB-08 fixture-tested.
</objective>
