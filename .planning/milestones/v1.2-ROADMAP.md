# Milestone v1.2: Neumorphic Redesign

**Status:** ✅ SHIPPED 2026-07-13

**Total Plans:** 28
**Git range:** v1.1..v1.2 (188 commits, 2026-06-20 → 2026-07-13)

## Overview

v1.2 transformed My Home from a stock-SwiftUI household finance hub into a polished, cohesive neumorphic (Soft UI) product. It began by building a single-source-of-truth design system (tokens, `NeuSurface` dual-shadow modifiers, `RollingMoneyText`, accessibility infrastructure), then mechanically restyled all nine screen groups to charcoal surfaces + canary-yellow accents with zero regressions. On top of that it shipped the design handoff's net-new surfaces: a "where it's going" spend donut on Overview (with tap-to-filter into Activity), a dedicated push-in Analytics screen (spending-trend area chart, by-category bars, inverted-color period delta chips, all fed by a single testable `AnalyticsAggregator`), and an on-device AI Insight card powered by Apple FoundationModels (iOS 26, two-layer availability gating, `@Generable` guided generation, numeric-integrity verification so the model never invents figures, streaming typewriter honoring Reduce Motion). Mid-milestone, light-mode support was promoted from backlog and delivered as Phase 17 — a full adaptive palette, reworked neumorphic shadow directions/opacities, non-glow treatment for the particle orb + activity rings, and a byte-identical dark-mode guarantee (DarkBitIdentityTests). All work stayed on zero new dependencies, iOS 17 deployment floor, no schema change, local-only.

## Requirements Outcome

28/29 requirements satisfied. The lone exception, **DS-03 (floating capsule tab bar)**, was implemented then intentionally reverted to the native `TabView` — a deferred design decision, not a defect (deep-link tab indices remained stable throughout). Phase 17 (light mode) was added after the requirements freeze and reverses the original "dark-mode-only" ground rule (DS-05) as an intentional scope addition. Milestone audit (`.planning/v1.2-MILESTONE-AUDIT.md`) closed as `tech_debt` — no functional blockers; integration/E2E verified empirically by building and running the app on two physical devices (iPhone 17 Pro Max, iPhone 15 Pro Max).
